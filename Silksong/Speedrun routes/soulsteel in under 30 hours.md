Getting Dash 

The start of the game up to Bell Beast should be intuitive. After Bellbeast, get the Craftmetal and Straight Pin in the next few rooms. The Craftmetal will be used to buy Magma Bell shortly, and Straight Pin is a good first red tool.

Getting Cloak

After getting dash, head to Hunter’s March instead of fighting Lace and buy the Fractured Mask. Shakra will assist you in the Hunter’s March arena as long as you haven’t entered Greymoor, Shellwood, or Bellhart. Before you leave, get the Memory Locket from the corpse near Savage Beastfly.

Before fighting Lace, buy and equip Magma Bell from Forge Daughter. There is an enemy rush guarding her, but it is very easy. If you got the Memory Locket in Hunter’s March, you can equip both Fractured Mask and Magma Bell. Magma Bell will help with the lava in Far Fields and fire-based bosses later on. Keep in mind it has a cooldown of 5-ish seconds.

Getting Cling Grip

After getting cloak and beating Fourth Chorus, get Warding Bell just left of the arena. Warding Bell is a good replacement for Magma Bell when it’s not being used. Instead of going to Greymoor, grind rosaries to buy a key for Wormways. This gives you access to Shellwood and lets you skip Moorwing. You can also skip Moorwing by getting five fleas to move the caravan if you prefer. Before exiting Wormways, get the Steel Soul exclusive satchel tool just before the top exit to increase your tool capacity (for Plasmium and Cogflies later).

You can get more tool capacity from tool pouch upgrades, like from Mort. Mort is a bit pricy, so come back for his upgrade later if you don’t have the rosaries. The crafting kit upgrades come later as I don’t focus on red tools for damage until I get Cogflies. If you use an offensive red tool build, you can get those upgrades earlier.

Getting Needle 2

After Cling Grip, prep for the Widow fight by getting Plasmium and a 6th mask. The extra hp from Plasmium helps not just for bosses but for any emergency situation that puts you low on masks. When using plasmium vs multi-phase bosses like Widow and Last Judge, wait to use it until the last phase. Refills are 50 rosaries, so use it sparingly. Get the Memory Locket in Wormways so you can equip it. 

For the 6th mask, either grind rosaries for the Bonebottom Mask Shard or fight the Deep Docks enemy rush. The enemy rush isn’t too difficult, but I listed Bonebottom as that is safer. If you’re comfortable with Widow, you can get the 6th mask from Weavernest Alta after the fight instead.

Then get the first pouch upgrade from Mort. This will increase your red tool capacity, like for Plasmium and Cogflies later on. If you have Shell Satchel equipped, you’ll get a fifth Plasmium with this upgrade. Otherwise, you’ll still have three Plasmium as the tool scaling sometimes doesn’t increase for low capacity tools.

Getting to Last Judge

For this phase, I prefer to fight Last Judge instead of Phantom since there’s a Craftmetal on the way and I find Last Judge easier, especially with Magma Bell. To prep, get the pouch upgrade from the shooting minigame, which becomes available after beating Widow. After the Greymoor arena, get Threefold Pin up and right from the arena. I prefer Threefold Pin over Straight Pin, but if you like Straight Pin more, head straight to the Blasted Steps.

In the first Blasted Steps room with enemies, there is a Memory Locket at the end of the room. This is the last Memory Locket you need to fill the Hunter’s Crest. The dice can be obtained through Luck’s minigame or from his corpse after beating Cogwork Dancers in Act 2. Lastly, get the Craftmetal near the end of the room before Last Judge for getting Cogflies later.

Needle 3 + Upgrades

This will be the longest act by far. I grouped the tool pouch upgrades together, but if you’re short on rosaries, you can grind enemies for rosaries or buy them later. My goto grinding spot is the top of Chambers with the large choir enemies.

Enter Choral Chambers instead of the Underworks since the Underworks doesn’t have notable items or abilities until you reach the Clawline path. Once you get to Chambers, fight Cogwork Dancers, open the Bellway, and get the dice from Luck’s corpse if you don’t yet have it.

In Cogwork Core, avoid the Spool Fragment in the bottom right as it’s guarded by a difficult miniboss you can fight later (Cogwork Clapper). While working on your 7th mask, grab the Pale Oil in Vaults by doing a dive skip on the Scrollreader (big staff enemy). This gets you a second needle upgrade before the Trobbio fight. 

Also consider buying Multibinder. You’ll need to complete the Missing Courier and Lost Brother wishes to see it in the Bellhart shop. Multibinder can be bought in Act 1, but it requires a lot of rosary grinding due to its high price. Grinding rosaries is much faster in Act 2 with Choral Chambers, which is why I put it here.

Wings

After fighting Trobbio, proceed to Clawline and equip Magma Bell. You can equip Magma Bell at the 12th Architect bench, which will help with the lava on the path to Clawline. After the enemy rush, get the Vaults Melody.

Then go through Mount Fay for double jump. Double jump will help with getting a certain Mask Shard and for navigating through High Halls in the next section. If you haven’t done a 100% practice run, do a run-through of Mount Fay on your main save to regain familiarity with the rooms. Don’t forget the Mask Shard on the left of Mount Fay after getting wings!

Getting 8th Mask

Get Cogflies from High Halls and then buy the first three crafting kit upgrades. Cogflies are relatively cheap compared to Plasmium, so don’t be afraid to use them vs difficult enemies and bosses. They disappear after six hits rather than a certain amount of time, so the time you summon them isn’t too important. This segment is very rosary heavy, so you might not get all the upgrades immediately. Get all the crafting kit upgrades before fighting Groal though.

For the 8th mask, do NOT buy the Songclave Mask Shard last. Because getting a fourth Mask Shard restores all your masks, get the Mask Shard in Wisp Thicket or Blasted Steps last since they have threats nearby.

For the Blasted Steps Mask Shard, you normally need Silk Soar, but you can do a skip with Swift Step, Clawline, and the Faydown Cloak that isn’t too difficult. When you get to the last elevated platform at the left, dash jump off it, Clawline at peak height, then double jump to the next platform. Once you get in the Mask Shard room, clear out the two enemies at the start (I died from forgetting to do this), watch for falling rocks, and go for the shard. Using Cogflies also helps clear out the enemies.

For the Wisp Thicket Mask Shard, start by getting the bench and equipping Magma Bell, which will lessen the damage from the wisp attacks. Don’t enter the Mask Shard room until you beat both Wisp lanterns in the first room. You can return to the bench if you mess up. In the Mask Shard room, you can beat the Burning Bugs one by one or run past them. If you have Plasmium to spare and choose this as your 4th mask, use the run past approach. Otherwise, use Cogflies to help beat them, and run back to the bench if you need to reset. Wait to fight Father of the Flame until Act 3 since he doesn’t turn void.

Needle 4

Next is the ingredient needle upgrade. I prefer getting this upgrade before the 9th mask since the extra damage helps with the lava escape Mask Shard and Savage Beastfly 2 fight. The wish requires Crustnut in Sands of Karak, Moss Stew from the Moss Druid, the Rasher from the brothers, Nectar in Halfway Home for 480 rosaries, and Mackmaggot from Chef Lugoli. You’ll need to fight Double Conchflies on the way to Sands of Karak.

Before you fight Chef Lugoli, head to the Ducts to get the Wreath of Purity and Apostate Key. The Wreath helps for Bilewater and makes the Chef Lugoli fight easier. The key gives you access to a Mask Shard from the Slab, letting you get nine masks before fighting Groal.

You should also reach 100 journal entries around this time, which lets you get a pouch upgrade from Nuu. If you have Shell Satchell equipped, this pouch upgrade won’t actually increase your Plasmium, but it will give you one more Cogfly.

Bilewater Prep

Next are Mask Shards. Use Magma Bell for both the lava escape Mask Shard and Savage Beastfly 2. Replace Multibinder with it and re-equip Multibinder after. You can flee from Savage Beastfly 2 if you have a rough start, but I advise against this since it’s surrounded by lava. Then get the third Mask Shard in the slab. You can fight First Sinner after getting the keys, who won’t kill your Steel Soul run, as shown by this post, https://www.reddit.com/r/Silksong/comments/1nj5tak/psa_some_bosses_do_not_kill_steel_soul_runs/, or you can Google “psa some bosses do not kill steel soul”.

Work on unlocking the Trail’s End wish. Finishing this wish lets Shakra assist you in the High Halls arena. Shakra’s master location is also somewhat of a pain to get to later, so it’s best to finish this wish along the way in Bilewater. You’ll need to visit any Shakra locations you’ve missed to unlock all her maps. Shakra sells 14 maps total. If you haven’t bought any maps and Shakra has 13 maps in her shop, the Bilewater map is all that you’re missing. You’ll also need two melodies to unlock the wish. At the top of the Cogwork melody, you can skip the enemies by wall jumping off the left wall.

Lastly, get 22 fleas to get the last pouch upgrade. This takes a bit of time, but I consider Groal the hardest part of the run, so you won’t regret getting this. You’ll get one more Plasmium and one more Cogfly with this upgrade.

Bilewater

Before entering Bilewater, equip Wreath of Purity. Keep in mind Wreath of Purity will break after a total time in muckmaggots has been reached, but it refreshes at benches. You’ll need to enter from Vaults since the Sinner’s Road path will block you from Twisted Bud. For a quick path, enter Bilewater and get the Twisted Bud, buy all the maps from Shakra, go to Bellhart for the Trail’s End wish, return to Bilewater, get the Mask Shard, complete the Trail’s End wish, then fight Groal.

For the Groal fight, use great slashes to kill the enemy waves, and don’t use Plasmium or Cogflies until Groal shows up. I use a strategy where you stay in the muckmaggots while he uses fireballs, then come out and great slash him after. Keep in mind you can’t charge great slashes while in muckmaggots. When he uses an attack that isn’t fireballs, wall jump spam in the corner to keep Wreath of Purity active for as long as possible.

Act 3 Prep

You can fight Lace 2 after Bilewater, but also fight the Forebrothers and Double Moss Mothers. The enemies the Forebrothers summon turn void in Act 3, and the Double Moss mothers turn void themselves in Act 3. You can find a list of the bosses that turn void at the bottom of this post, but the required ones are already listed here in Act 2. 

Then work on missing crests, Spool Fragments, Vesticrest upgrades, Tool Kit upgrades, and fleas before starting Act 3. The vesticrest upgrades require a certain number of open tool slots and silk skill slots across all your crests except Hunter’s Crest. This means you will have 0 slots unlocked until you get a second crest. You need 12 slots for the yellow tool upgrade and 20 slots for the blue tool upgrade. You can unlock the vesticrest earlier if you’re missing important tools for your build and you’re fine with grinding Memory Lockets. Also, get all the fleas in Act 2 so you can quickly get the last needle upgrade in Act 3 before fighting Bell Eater.

Unlocking Silk and Soul for Act 3 is based on a point system and a few other requirements. The lists below are from the Silksong Wiki and show the wishes involved. You'll need to complete the required wishes and get 17 quest points. The delivery quest wishes only count points once. You’ll also need to buy a Bellhome in Bellhart, then move the fleacart to Fleatopia and fight Lace 2 if you haven't already. To get the Bellhome, complete the Restoration of Bellhart and Bellhart’s Glory wishes and find Tipp and Pill if you haven’t done so. When you complete Restoration of Bellhart, talk to Pavo, leave Bellhart, and when you return, Bellhart’s Glory will be available.

Needle 5 + Last Mask

Act 3 is relatively easy since the dream bosses don’t kill you, but the enemy rush at the start is very chokable. I chose to save quit when you wake up with four masks to get full masks before the fight, but respect if you do it without. You’ll lose all your tools, so rely on great slash between waves and memorize the enemy attacks beforehand.

Start the Flea Festival for the last Pale Oil and fight Bell Eater once you get the last needle upgrade. While this boss is optional, the Bell Beast helps a ton with moving around. You can also wait to fight Bell Eater till the 10th mask or fight it as soon as you start Act 3 depending on your confidence level (or skip it entirely if you prefer).

Then get the Plasmium Gland from the Advanced Alchemy wish, which becomes available in Act 3. This way, you never have to pay for extra Plasmium again. 

Continue by entering the Abyss and getting Silk Soar. Then fight Karmelita and get the Sprintmaster Mask Shard to unlock the Hidden Hunter wish in Bellhart. After fighting Gurr for the wish, get the two Mask Shards from the Dark Hearts wish and Brightvein for the final mask.

Last Required Bosses

Then comes the last chokable bosses in the game (besides Lost Lace), which are required for 100%. The list starts with The Unravelled who in my opinion is the hardest, and ends with Green Prince, who is the easiest since he is in a dream. Keep in mind you can’t really heal during Father of the Flame since he doesn’t give silk. I put Summoned Savior on the list, despite it being optional, since it’s an easy fight with max upgrades and is a Steel Soul exclusive. For Pinstress, you can leave the fight if you’re struggling. This is the only required boss where fleeing is a safe option, so if you’d like, practice exiting the arena as soon as the battle starts so you know where to land.

I normally would have put Seth at the top of this list, but you can skip him if you go through Verdania. To unlock Verdania, free the Green Prince in Sinner’s Road with a simple key, talk to him above the left Ventrica in Choral Chambers and left of the Cogwork Dancers arena, then meet him at the right edge of Greymoor.

Everything Else

Once you finish the bosses, focus on tools, Memory Lockets for binding Eva, and missing tools for the remaining percent. Everbloom also counts as a percent. Getting every Memory Locket is not required to Bind Eva, but you will need most of them. Last is Lost Lace, which is a doable fight if you practice on your main save and have max upgrades. I focus on parry for the fight and use it against most of her attacks.
