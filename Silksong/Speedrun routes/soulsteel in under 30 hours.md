### Act 1 Strategy Notes

**Getting Cloak**: After getting dash, head to Hunter's March instead of fighting Lace and buy the Fractured Mask. Shakra will assist you in the Hunter's March arena as long as you haven't entered Greymoor, Shellwood, or other late areas. The Memory Locket obtained here will be useful later.

**Magma Bell Setup**: Before fighting Lace, buy and equip Magma Bell from Forge Daughter. There's an enemy rush guarding her, but it's very manageable. If you already obtained the Memory Locket in Hunter's March, you can equip both Fractured Mask and Magma Bell for the fight.

**Getting Cling Grip**: After getting cloak and defeating Fourth Chorus, grab Warding Bell just to the left of the arena. Warding Bell is an excellent replacement for Magma Bell when it's not being used. Instead of heading to Greymoor immediately, grind rosaries for the Wormways Key to skip Moorwing.

**Tool Pouch Upgrades**: Mort offers increased tool capacity upgrades. These are expensive early on, so return for his upgrades later if you're short on rosaries. Crafting kit upgrades come later when you have more resources.

**Preparing for Widow**: After Cling Grip, prep for the Widow fight by getting Plasmium and a 6th mask. The extra HP from Plasmium is crucial not just for bosses but for any emergency situation that puts you low on masks.

**6th Mask Options**: Either grind rosaries for the Bonebottom Mask Shard or fight the Deep Docks enemy rush. The enemy rush isn't overly difficult, but Bonebottom is the safer option. If you're comfortable with combat, the rush is faster.

**First Pouch Upgrade**: Get the first pouch upgrade from Mort. This increases your red tool capacity for items like Plasmium and Cogflies later. If you have Shell Satchel equipped, you'll get a fifth Plasmium with this upgrade.

**Last Judge vs. Phantom**: For this phase, it's better to fight Last Judge instead of Phantom because there's a Craftmetal on the way and Last Judge is generally easier, especially with Magma Bell equipped.

**Completing Hunter's Crest**: In the first Blasted Steps room with enemies, there's a Memory Locket at the end. This is the final Memory Locket you need to complete the Hunter's Crest. The Dice can be obtained through Luck or by exploring certain areas.

### Act 2 Strategy Notes

**Choral Chambers Entry**: Enter Choral Chambers instead of the Underworks since the Underworks doesn't have significant items or abilities until you reach the Clawline path. Once in Chambers, fight Cogwork Dancers, unlock the Spool Fragment, and continue with your mask collection.

**Cogwork Core Strategy**: Avoid the Spool Fragment in the bottom right of Cogwork Core—it's guarded by a difficult miniboss (Cogwork Clapper) that you can fight later. While working on your 7th mask, grab the Pale Oil in Vaults.

**Multibinder Consideration**: Consider buying Multibinder if you have resources. You'll need to complete the "Missing Courier" and "Lost Brother" wishes to see it in the Bellhart shop. Multibinder can technically be bought in Act 1, but it requires a lot of rosaries.

**Post-Trobbio Strategy**: After fighting Trobbio, proceed to Clawline and equip Magma Bell. You can equip Magma Bell at the 12th Architect bench, which helps with the lava on the path to Clawline. After the enemy rush, get Clawline and continue.

**Mount Fay Navigation**: Go through Mount Fay for double jump. Double jump will help with obtaining a specific Mask Shard and navigating High Halls in the next section. If you haven't done a 100% practice run, this segment can be tricky.

**Getting 8th Mask Strategy**: Get Cogflies from High Halls and then buy the first three crafting kit upgrades. Cogflies are relatively cheap compared to Plasmium, so don't hesitate to use them against difficult enemies and bosses. This act is the longest by far.

**8th Mask Collection Order**: Do NOT buy the Songclave Mask Shard last. Since obtaining a fourth Mask Shard restores all your masks, get the Mask Shard in Wisp Thicket or Blasted Steps last since they have threatening enemies.

**Blasted Steps Skip**: For the Blasted Steps Mask Shard, you normally need Silk Soar, but you can perform a skip with Swift Step, Clawline, and the Faydown Cloak. This skip isn't overly difficult but requires some practice.

**Wisp Thicket Strategy**: For Wisp Thicket, start by getting the bench and equipping Magma Bell to reduce damage from wisp attacks. Don't enter the Mask Shard room until you've defeated both Wisp lanterns nearby.

**Wreath of Purity**: Before fighting Chef Lugoli, head to the Ducts to get the Wreath of Purity and Apostate Key. The Wreath helps significantly for Bilewater and makes the Chef Lugoli fight much easier.

**100 Journal Milestone**: You should reach 100 journal entries around this time, which unlocks a pouch upgrade from Nuu. If you have Shell Satchel equipped, this pouch upgrade won't increase your Plasmium count.

**Bilewater Prep**: Use Magma Bell for both the lava escape Mask Shard and Savage Beastfly 2. Replace Multibinder with it and re-equip Multibinder after. You can flee from Savage Beastfly 2 if health gets too low.

**Trail's End Wish**: Work on unlocking the Trail's End wish. Finishing this wish lets Shakra assist you in the High Halls arena. Shakra's master location is somewhat difficult to reach later, so it's best to complete this now.

**Flea Collection**: Lastly, get 22 fleas for the final pouch upgrade. This takes time, but Groal is considered the hardest fight in the run, so the extra Plasmium is worth it.

**Bilewater Fight Prep**: Before entering Bilewater, equip Wreath of Purity. Keep in mind the Wreath will break after a certain time in muckmaggots, but it refreshes at benches. You'll need to enter from the correct path.

**Groal Strategy**: For the Groal fight, use great slashes to kill enemy waves. Don't use Plasmium or Cogflies until Groal appears. A good strategy is to stay in the muckmaggots while Groal uses fireballs, then strike during openings.

### Act 3 Strategy Notes

**Act 3 Difficulty**: Act 3 is relatively forgiving since dream bosses don't kill you. However, the enemy rush at the start is very chokable. It's recommended to save-quit when you wake up with four masks to restore full masks before the fight.

**Bell Eater & Fast Travel**: Start the Flea Festival for the last Pale Oil and fight Bell Eater once you get the last needle upgrade. While this boss is optional, Bell Beast helps tremendously with movement and navigation.

**Plasmium Gland**: Get the Plasmium Gland from the Advanced Alchemy wish, which becomes available in Act 3. This way, you never have to pay for extra Plasmium again.

**Abyss & Silk Soar**: Continue by entering the Abyss and obtaining Silk Soar. Then fight Karmelita and get the Sprintmaster Mask Shard to unlock the Hidden Hunter wish in Bellhart.

**Final Boss Strategy**: The final chokable bosses (besides Lost Lace) are the required fights for 100%. The Unravelled is considered the hardest, while Green Prince is the easiest.

**Verdania Skip for Seth**: You can normally skip Seth by going through Verdania. To unlock Verdania, free the Green Prince in Sinner's Road with a simple key, talk to him about cooperation, and progress through the area.

**Memory Lockets & Eva**: Once you finish the bosses, focus on collecting tools, Memory Lockets for binding Eva, and missing tools for the remaining completion percentage. Everbloom also counts toward completion.
