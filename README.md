# HK-speedrun
strategy for hollow knight and silksong

## Introduction — Hollow Knight
This repository collects strategies, routes, and practical tips for speedrunning Hollow Knight. The goal is to provide concise, actionable guidance for runners at different skill levels — from beginners learning movement and combat to experienced players optimizing route and glitches.

Whether you're aiming for Any% or 100% runs, the notes below focus on consistency, key movement techniques, and fight strategies that reduce time and improve survival.

## Hollow Knight: Core Strategy and Tips
- Focus on movement early: nail down dashing, nail-bouncing, and air control. Smooth traversal saves more time than small combat optimizations.
- Learn and practice the following movement tactics:
  - Dash-cancel and dash-aim to quickly cross rooms and skip enemy encounters.
  - Nail-bounce to maintain momentum and recover health when necessary.
  - Use wall-jumping and precise platforming to shorten routes.
- Combat priorities:
  - Prioritize hits that let you keep moving; avoid long stagger animations when routing.
  - Learn enemy patterns for key rooms (e.g., Sentries, Hornet fights, Mantis Lords) to minimize damage and stun time.
  - Use ranged spells (Vengeful Spirit / Shade Soul) to safely handle grouped enemies.
- Boss fights:
  - Study safe windows for damage and when to reposition. Conserving health prevents time loss from detours to benches or collecting Mask Shards.
  - Practice consistent damage patterns and when to use healing during multi-phase fights.
- Resource routing:
  - Plan which charms to equip for each segment — choose movement and survivability over raw damage in many routing contexts.
  - Note bench and stag station locations on your route to optimize map usage.

## Practical Tips for Speedrunning
- Warm up movement and basic combat before runs — muscle memory is critical.
- Record runs and review mistakes to find consistent improvements.
- Use practice segments (splits) to isolate and improve difficult sections.
- Optimize loading/transition paths (stops at benches, stag stations) to shave seconds off repeated runs.
- Stay calm and prioritize consistency over risky time-saving tricks until you're confident.
